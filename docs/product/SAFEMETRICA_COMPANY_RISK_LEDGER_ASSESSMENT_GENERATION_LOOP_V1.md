# SafeMetrica Company Risk Ledger & Assessment Generation Loop v1

## 1. Purpose

This document defines the long-term product structure for turning SafeMetrica into a living company-specific safety data system.

The goal is not to replace legal judgment or automatically finalize a risk assessment.

The goal is to help each company build and maintain a practical company risk ledger that can support:

- regular risk assessment candidates
- ad-hoc risk assessment candidates
- worker participation review
- near miss and incident signal reflection
- improvement measure suggestions
- before and after evidence connection
- monthly reporting
- future Physical Safety AI OS expansion

SafeMetrica should become more practical than generic templates by reflecting each company's real work, equipment, environment, worker signals, and improvement history.

## 2. Core Position

SafeMetrica is not a legal-compliance guarantee, penalty-prevention service, risk assessment outsourcing service, safety management agency service, or no-accident guarantee.

SafeMetrica's safe operating position:

- AI suggests candidates.
- Managers review and edit.
- Business owners confirm.
- The system records the process and evidence.

## 3. Product Vision

SafeMetrica should evolve from a field input app into a living safety operating data system.

The final structure:

1. collect company-specific safety signals
2. classify hazards by major, middle, and minor categories
3. separate common and company-specific hazards
4. connect hazards to work types, equipment, site environment, and worker participation
5. generate risk assessment candidates
6. support manager review and business owner confirmation
7. connect improvement measures and evidence photos
8. carry unresolved or repeated hazards into the next assessment cycle

## 4. Hazard Source Layers

Company risk ledger candidates may come from these sources.

### 4.1 Industry Common Hazards

Examples:

- construction waste handling
- living waste collection
- logistics and warehouse work
- manufacturing equipment operation
- vehicle and heavy equipment movement
- loading and unloading
- lifting and manual handling
- fire and explosion risk
- heat illness and weather risk

### 4.2 Company-Specific Hazards

Examples:

- site layout
- traffic routes
- equipment arrangement
- storage method
- work sequence
- subcontractor interface
- night work pattern
- narrow passage
- dust, noise, odor, slippery floor, or poor visibility
- repeated worker reports

### 4.3 Equipment and Machinery Hazards

Examples:

- forklift
- excavator
- crusher
- conveyor
- press
- CNC machine
- crane
- loader
- sorting machine
- compacting equipment
- vehicle tailgate or loading box

### 4.4 Environment and Site Hazards

Examples:

- floor condition
- slope
- blind spot
- vehicle and pedestrian mixed route
- weather exposure
- heat, cold, wind, rain, snow
- poor lighting
- confined or narrow space
- dust or flying object
- noise and vibration

### 4.5 Worker Participation Signals

Examples:

- share confirmation
- anonymous risk report
- near miss report
- improvement suggestion
- worker representative confirmation
- manager review memo
- repeated no-action signal
- monthly summary follow-up

### 4.6 Incident and Near Miss History

Examples:

- near miss
- minor injury
- first-aid case
- property damage
- repeated unsafe condition
- repeated unsafe behavior
- delayed improvement

## 5. Classification Structure

Each hazard candidate should be classified by:

- major category
- middle category
- minor category
- industry
- work type
- equipment
- site or area
- hazard factor
- possible consequence
- existing control
- improvement measure candidate
- evidence status
- review status
- assessment reflection status

Example classification:

- Major: vehicle and heavy equipment
- Middle: forklift route
- Minor: pedestrian and forklift mixed movement
- Site: warehouse entrance
- Signal source: worker report and manager review
- Assessment candidate: regular assessment and ad-hoc assessment
- Evidence: before photo, after photo
- Review status: manager review needed

## 6. Company Risk Ledger

The company risk ledger is the company-specific source of truth for hazard candidates.

It should store:

- company code
- site name
- hazard title
- major category
- middle category
- minor category
- work type
- equipment or machinery
- site environment
- source type
- source record reference
- worker feedback summary
- representative confirmation status
- manager review status
- improvement measure
- responsible person or role
- due date
- before evidence
- after evidence
- assessment reflection status
- next review cycle

The company risk ledger should not automatically become a confirmed risk assessment table without human review.

## 7. Common and Non-Common Hazard Separation

SafeMetrica should separate hazards into:

### 7.1 Common Hazards

Common hazards are industry or work-type hazards that appear across many similar companies.

Examples:

- forklift collision
- vehicle reversing
- falling object
- manual handling injury
- slip and trip
- heat illness
- fire risk

### 7.2 Company-Specific Hazards

Company-specific hazards are created by the actual site condition, equipment layout, work sequence, worker reports, and incident history.

Examples:

- specific blind spot near a site gate
- specific crusher maintenance point
- repeated worker report about one route
- recurring heat exposure at one work zone
- repeated loading area congestion

## 8. Assessment Generation Loop

The SafeMetrica assessment generation loop should work as follows.

1. collect baseline industry hazard candidates
2. collect company setup data
3. collect worker participation signals
4. collect manager review results
5. collect representative confirmation signals
6. collect evidence photos and action records
7. create company risk ledger candidates
8. classify candidates by major, middle, and minor categories
9. suggest regular or ad-hoc assessment candidates
10. manager reviews and edits
11. business owner confirms final use
12. selected items are reflected in the assessment table
13. improvement measures are assigned
14. before and after evidence is connected
15. unresolved or repeated hazards return to the next cycle

## 9. Regular and Ad-Hoc Assessment Candidates

### 9.1 Regular Assessment Candidates

Regular assessment candidates may include:

- company common hazards
- industry common hazards
- equipment hazards
- repeated worker reports
- unresolved previous hazards
- annual review items
- high-frequency work type hazards

### 9.2 Ad-Hoc Assessment Candidates

Ad-hoc assessment candidates may include:

- new equipment
- new process
- new site condition
- incident or near miss
- repeated anonymous report
- worker representative objection
- major environmental change
- urgent manager review signal

## 10. Evidence Photo Connection

SafeMetrica should connect evidence photos to the risk assessment loop.

Evidence types:

- before photo
- action photo
- after photo
- worker report photo
- near miss photo
- equipment photo
- site condition photo
- monthly report evidence

Assessment table connection:

- hazard item
- existing condition
- improvement measure
- action status
- before evidence
- after evidence
- review date
- next cycle status

Photos must not be treated as automatic proof of completion without manager review.

## 11. Improvement Measure Support

SafeMetrica may suggest improvement measure candidates based on:

- hazard category
- equipment type
- site condition
- prior actions
- repeated reports
- monthly review
- industry common measures

Improvement measures are candidates.

Managers must review, edit, and confirm actual measures.

## 12. Worker Participation Reflection

Worker participation should affect the company risk ledger through:

- share confirmation count
- anonymous report count
- near miss count
- improvement suggestion count
- repeated signal count
- representative confirmation
- representative objection or follow-up opinion
- manager review result

Anonymous reports can become hazard candidates, but they must not expose worker identity in customer-facing outputs.

## 13. Manager Review and Owner Confirmation

SafeMetrica should separate:

- AI candidate suggestion
- manager review
- owner confirmation
- final assessment reflection

The system should record:

- who reviewed
- when reviewed
- what changed
- what was accepted
- what was rejected
- what was deferred
- what was reflected in the next assessment

## 14. KRAS Boundary

SafeMetrica may reference public risk factor classification concepts and industry safety knowledge.

Do not describe SafeMetrica as legally replacing KRAS or any official risk assessment process.

Recommended wording:

SafeMetrica helps create company-specific risk assessment candidates by combining industry hazard categories, equipment, work environment, worker participation, near misses, improvement records, and evidence photos.

Avoid wording:

- KRAS replacement
- legal risk assessment completion
- automatic compliance
- AI final assessment
- penalty prevention
- legal immunity

## 17. Current Decision

The company risk ledger and assessment generation loop are feasible.

The correct next step is not an automatic final risk assessment generator.

The correct next step is to build a reviewed candidate system:

- AI proposes
- manager reviews
- owner confirms
- system records
- next assessment cycle reflects the result

This is the practical path for SafeMetrica to become a living safety data system.
